About Me

I am an R&D Engineer specializing in mathematical modeling, numerical solutions, and complex multiphysics simulations. With a strong foundation in finite volume methods and turbulence modeling, I bridge the gap between theoretical algorithms and industrial fluid dynamics.

Previously at Tata Steel, I developed custom C++ solvers within the OpenFOAM framework, building a comprehensive Blast furnace Simulator (BlaSim). This involved formulating numerical solutions for mass, momentum, energy, and species transport across solid-gas-liquid multiphase flows with intricate reaction kinetics.

My PhD research at IIT Delhi leveraged advanced numerical schemes to simulate multi-element high-lift airfoils, achieving a 78% lift improvement and significant aeroacoustic noise reduction through optimized geometry and precise control of shear-layer interactions.
